About Me

I am a fourth-year PhD candidate in Accounting at NUS Business School, expected to graduate in June 2026. My research examines the intersection of regulatory policy and corporate innovation, with a focus on how tax policies and regulatory frictions affect product market strategies and firm innovation.
I will be a visiting scholar at UNC Chapel Hill's Kenan-Flagler Business School in Fall 2026.

Regulatory Frictions and Product Innovation: Evidence from Consumer Markets
Job Market Paper
This paper examines how regulatory frictions affect firm innovation decisions using comprehensive Nielsen consumer data. Employing survival analysis and entropy balancing methods, I document systematic patterns in product entry, survival, and market dynamics.
